Benefit from Our Rich History

In 1969, astronaut Neil Armstrong became the first person to set foot on the Moon, gas peaked at $0.35 per gallon, and IGI began its photomask operations.

Over the next 46 yearsÂ we became a global leader in the precision imaging industry, and developed the software that continues today. Click through the timeline for highlights in our company’s history.

+ Expand All

1969

IGI was founded to offer reprographics services to the engineering and architectural communities

1973

IGI develops high tolerance glass tooling to solve the manufacturing problems of mainframe and high speed PCB industries. IGI concentrates on providing those services.

1976

Introduced precision digital imaging on film and glass for the printed circuit board industry.

1978

Introduced grayscale imaging for displays and filters.

1983

IGI introduces its first PC based CAD/CAM system for precision graphics applications.

1987

Upgraded film imaging to digital laser photoplotting.

1992

Upgrades large area glass mask imaging with direct write capability.

1998

Improves large area mask direct write and hard surface mask graphics capabilities to accommodate micro geometry

1999

IGI expands to Colorado Springs

2001

Creates a large area mask facility in Singapore to service the fast-growing electronics market in Asia.
Pioneers large area automated optical inspection, (AOI).

2002

Wrote rasterizing software to provide CAD reference AOI, and pioneered its use for large area masks. Established OEM distribution for plotter and AOI front end software.

2004

Obtains leading edge direct write imager. Consolidates production facilities in Minneapolis

2005

Expanded CAD reference AOI capabilities to meet tighter tolerances and provide better defect detection.

2007

Installs a new 3D system for sculpted surfaces.

2008

Develops the X-301 mask coating to reduce stiction and extend the life of photomasks.

2010

IGI Introduces Phototooling Toolbox software, a full featured hierarchical CAD/CAM system for precision graphic applications software.

2012

IGI Introduces Gen Stencil solder paste stencil software to help Stencil Manufacturers and EMS companies dramatically accelerate data preparation to ensure solder paste stencil repeatability resulting in faster delivery times, increased yields and ultimately increased profit margin.

2015

IGI introduces the NanoSculpt 3D Solution Suite that provides our customers with advanced nano/microstructure technologies and techniques. IGI supplies shims, molds, and inserts which are used for embossing, injection molding, holographic imaging, and other applications for use in the MEMS, medical, electronics, semiconductor, security, and other industries.

2016

Photronics, Inc. purchased manufacturing assets and certain intellectual property from Infinite Graphics on Nov. 1, 2016. After assisting with the transition of assets, Infinite Graphics resumes development in their CAD/CAM software business, focusing on Gen Stencil, EXT and RIP/OEM products.

2017

IGI offers a free download version of their well established CAD/CAM software -- IGI PT Viewer. This download features the same easy import of RS-274X, DXF/DWG, GDSII, ODB++, and other data formats. Viewing features such as finding zero width geometry, calculating areas/perimeters, viewing hierarchy, measurements and counts, provide advanced tools for a view-only application.
